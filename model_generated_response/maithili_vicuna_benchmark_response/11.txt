                        Response in English: Using a single-use plastic bottle has a negative impact on the environment and human health. Single-use plastic bottles are made from non-biodegradable plastic, which takes hundreds of years to decompose. As a result, they end up in landfills, oceans, and other natural habitats, harming wildlife and polluting the environment. Single-use plastic bottles also release toxic chemicals that can leach into the groundwater and contaminate drinking water sources.

On the other hand, using a reusable bottle has a positive impact on the environment and human health. Reusable bottles are made from materials such as stainless steel, glass, or BPA-free plastic, which can be recycled or reused many times. This reduces the amount of plastic waste that ends up in landfills, oceans, and other natural habitats. Additionally, reusable bottles do not release toxic chemicals that can contaminate drinking water sources.

In summary, using a single-use plastic bottle has a negative impact on the environment and human health, while using a reusable bottle has a positive impact on both..
