                        Response in English: The government can use fiscal and monetary policies to combat an economic downturn. Fiscal policy refers to the use of government spending and taxation to influence the economy. During an economic downturn, the government can increase spending on infrastructure, social programs, and tax cuts to stimulate the economy and create jobs. Monetary policy, on the other hand, refers to the actions of the central bank to influence the money supply and interest rates. During an economic downturn, the central bank can lower interest rates to encourage borrowing and spending, and can also purchase government bonds to increase the money supply. Both fiscal and monetary policies can be used together to combat an economic downturn..
