                        Response in English: CRISPR-Cas9 is a gene editing technology that allows scientists to make precise changes to the DNA of living organisms. The process involves two main components: a guide RNA and an enzyme called Cas9. The guide RNA is designed to recognize and bind to a specific sequence of DNA, while Cas9 acts as a molecular scissors, cutting the DNA at the targeted location. This creates a double-stranded break in the DNA, which the cell's natural repair mechanisms attempt to fix by inserting or deleting genetic material. By carefully choosing the guide RNA sequence, scientists can target specific genes and make precise changes to the organism's genome..
