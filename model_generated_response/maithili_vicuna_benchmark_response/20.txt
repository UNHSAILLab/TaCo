Instruction in English:  Explain how the process of natural selection contributes to the evolution and adaptation of species..
                        Response in English: Natural selection is the process by which organisms that are better adapted to their environment are more likely to survive and reproduce, while those that are less well-adapted are less likely to survive and reproduce. Over time, this process can lead to changes in the genetic makeup of a population, resulting in the evolution of new traits and the adaptation of species to their environment. For example, if a population of birds living in a cold climate develops a mutation that allows them to grow thicker feathers, those birds with thicker feathers will be better able to survive the cold and reproduce, while birds with thinner feathers will be less likely to survive and reproduce. Over time, the thicker-feathered birds will become more common in the population, and the thinner-feathered birds will become less common. This is an example of how natural selection can contribute to the evolution and adaptation of species..
