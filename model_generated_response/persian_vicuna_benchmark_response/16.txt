                        Response in English: CRISPR-Cas9 is a gene editing technology that allows scientists to make precise changes to an organism's DNA. It works by targeting a specific sequence of DNA and using an enzyme called Cas9 to cut the DNA at that location. This creates a break in the DNA that can be repaired by the cell's natural repair mechanisms, resulting in a permanent change to the genome. Potential applications of CRISPR-Cas9 include treating genetic diseases, improving agricultural crops, and developing new therapies for cancer. However, the ethical implications of this technology are complex, as it raises questions about the potential for designer babies and eugenics..
