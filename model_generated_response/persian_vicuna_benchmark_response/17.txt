Instruction in English:  How does vaccination work to protect people and communities against infectious diseases, and what is herd immunity? Your response must be less than 6 sentences..
                        Response in English: Vaccination works by stimulating the immune system to produce antibodies against a specific disease. When a person is vaccinated, their immune system produces antibodies that can recognize and neutralize the virus or bacteria that causes the disease. If the person is exposed to the disease in the future, the antibodies will prevent the virus or bacteria from causing illness. Herd immunity occurs when a high percentage of the population is vaccinated against a disease, making it difficult for the disease to spread. This protects those who are unable to be vaccinated, such as infants or people with weakened immune systems..
