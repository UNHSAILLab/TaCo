Instruction in English:  How does vaccination work to protect individuals and communities from infectious diseases, and what is herd immunity?.
                        Response in English: Vaccination works by stimulating the body's immune system to produce antibodies against a specific infectious agent, such as a virus or bacteria. When a person is vaccinated, their immune system recognizes the agent as foreign and produces antibodies to fight it. If the person is later exposed to the actual infectious agent, their body is already prepared to fight it off because of the antibodies produced during the vaccination.

Herd immunity, also known as community immunity, occurs when a large enough proportion of a population is vaccinated to prevent the spread of an infectious disease. When most people in a community are vaccinated, it becomes difficult for the disease to spread because there are fewer susceptible people to infect. This protects those who cannot be vaccinated, such as infants or people with weakened immune systems, as well as the community as a whole..
